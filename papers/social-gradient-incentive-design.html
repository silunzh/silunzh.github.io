Paper Overview

Incentive Design without Hypergradients: A Social-Gradient Method

G. Vasileiou, L. Zhang, and S. Zhang, 2026.

Takeaway: We update incentives through social-gradient information, avoiding direct hypergradient computations through strategic agents' equilibrium responses.

Why It Matters

Incentive design often becomes difficult because changing an incentive changes the equilibrium chosen by many strategic agents. Differentiating through that equilibrium map can be expensive, fragile, or unavailable. A hypergradient-free route makes adaptive incentive design more usable in data-limited networked systems.

Technical Contribution

  • Introduces a social-gradient viewpoint for improving planner objectives through incentive updates.
  • Avoids explicit hypergradient calculations through the agents' equilibrium response map.
  • Targets strategic multi-agent settings where preferences and responses must be learned from interaction.

Who Should Read This

Researchers in incentive design, game theory, bilevel optimization, online learning, control, mechanism design, and networked decision systems.
