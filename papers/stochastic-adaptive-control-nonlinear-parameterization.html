Paper Overview

Stochastic Adaptive Control for Systems with Nonlinear Parameterization: Almost Sure Stability and Tracking

L. Zhang, B. Wahlberg, and S. Zhang, 2026.

Takeaway: We study adaptive control for stochastic systems with nonlinear parameterization and establish almost-sure stability and tracking guarantees.

Why It Matters

Many physical and engineered systems must be controlled under uncertainty, noise, and model mismatch. When unknown parameters enter nonlinearly, classical adaptive control tools can become restrictive. Stability and tracking guarantees are especially important when learning and control happen at the same time.

Technical Contribution

  • Studies stochastic adaptive control for systems with nonlinear parameterization.
  • Establishes almost-sure stability and tracking properties under stochastic dynamics.
  • Contributes to the theoretical foundation for adaptive and learning-enabled control under uncertainty.

Who Should Read This

Researchers in adaptive control, stochastic systems, nonlinear control, learning-based control, robotics, and systems theory.
