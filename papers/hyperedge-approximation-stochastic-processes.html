Paper Overview

Hyperedge Approximation for Stochastic Processes on Higher-Order Networks

A. Sheng, A. McAvoy, Y. Tian, S. Zhang, A. Fontan, and J. B. Plotkin, 2026.

Takeaway: The paper develops an ℓ-hyperedge approximation for stochastic population processes in which both payoffs and state updates can depend on group interactions, not only pairwise links.

Why It Matters

Graphs are powerful, but many social, biological, and engineered systems are shaped by groups of three or more agents. This matters for cooperation, contagion, and collective behavior, where reducing a group interaction to pairwise links can miss the mechanism that drives the dynamics.

Technical Contribution

  • Extends classical pair approximation from graphs to regular hypergraphs with groups of size ℓ.
  • Handles higher-order interactions that determine payoffs and higher-order processes that update states.
  • Applies the framework to evolutionary games, including nonlinear public-goods interactions that have no pairwise reduction.
  • Derives closed-form insight for complex contagion and fixation probabilities when inheritance or transmission occurs within hyperedges.

Who Should Read This

Researchers in higher-order networks, stochastic population processes, evolutionary dynamics, cooperation, complex contagion, network science, and collective decision-making.
