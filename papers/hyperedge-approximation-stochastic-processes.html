Paper Overview

Hyperedge Approximation for Stochastic Processes on Higher-Order Networks

A. Sheng, A. McAvoy, Y. Tian, S. Zhang, A. Fontan, and J. B. Plotkin, 2026.

Takeaway: We approximate stochastic processes on higher-order networks with tractable hyperedge-level descriptions that retain key collective effects.

Why It Matters

Many biological, social, and engineered systems involve group interactions rather than only pairwise links. Exact stochastic models on higher-order networks can quickly become too large to analyze directly, so useful approximations are essential for understanding collective behavior at scale.

Technical Contribution

  • Develops a hyperedge-based approximation for stochastic processes on higher-order network structures.
  • Connects local stochastic update rules with lower-dimensional aggregate descriptions.
  • Provides a route toward scalable analysis and simulation of dynamics with non-pairwise interactions.

Who Should Read This

Researchers in higher-order networks, stochastic processes, evolutionary dynamics, network science, epidemics, social learning, and collective decision-making.
